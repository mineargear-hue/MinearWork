The Wire Stretcher

There is a tool called a wire stretcher. It does not exist. It has never existed. No manufacturer has ever produced one. No supply house has ever stocked one. And yet, on jobsites across America, every single year without exception, a fresh-faced apprentice is dispatched to find one with total confidence by someone who has been in the trade for three decades and is barely holding back a grin.

The wire stretcher is the cornerstone of jobsite mythology — the founding document of trade initiation. Its genius as a prank lies in its plausibility. It sounds real. And that, ultimately, is the entire point.

Launching Your Own
Author Store

This landing page is ready to go. Here's exactly how to customize it and connect it to payments and delivery so you can start selling your books and products.

01

Replace Placeholder Content

Edit the HTML file: swap in your real book titles, descriptions, prices, author bio, and chapter list. The structure is already built — just update the text and cover colors to match your brand.

Edit HTML
02

Upload to a Host

This is a single HTML file — host it anywhere. Best options: Netlify (free drag-and-drop), GitHub Pages (free, connects to your domain), or upload via your domain registrar's file manager.

Hosting
03

Connect Stripe for Payments

Create a free account at stripe.com. For each product, create a Payment Link inside Stripe. Replace each href="#" on the Buy Now buttons with your Stripe Payment Link URL. No backend needed.

Stripe
04

Set Up Digital Delivery

For digital products (PDFs, ebooks), use Gumroad or SendOwl. Upload your files there, get product links, and point your buy buttons to those links. Both handle secure file delivery automatically after purchase.

Delivery
05

Wire Up the Email Form

Create a free account at Mailchimp or ConvertKit. Replace the subscribe form with their embed code, or use Formspree to forward submissions to your email. Takes 10 minutes.

Email Marketing
06

Add Your Domain

Buy a domain at Namecheap or Google Domains (~$12/year). Point it to your host via the DNS settings. Most hosts have a one-click "add custom domain" option. Your store is now live at your own URL.

Domain
07

Add a Real Author Photo

Replace the ⚡ emoji in the portrait frame with an <img> tag pointing to your actual photo. A professional headshot or on-site photo works best. Keep it honest — this is a trade book.

Content
08

Track Sales & Traffic

Add Google Analytics (free) by pasting one script tag before </head>. Connect Stripe's dashboard for revenue tracking. You'll know exactly what's selling and where traffic comes from within 48 hours.
